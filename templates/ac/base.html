Modern computation generates data -- lots and lots of data. Applying the results of computation towards the pursuit of scientific insight is challenged by the demanding task of synthesizing vast datasets into fundamental understanding. Unrivaled in its ability to produce not only insight but also beauty: a single image, revealing as a distillate what otherwise remains impenetrable and hidden. 'A picture is worth a thousand words' becomes 'a picture is worth a hundred terabytes'.

In the process of visualization many aesthetic choices must be made. While an image from a computer simulation or a mammoth dataset can be functional, it can also be beautiful. Astrophysics in particular is a rich discipline for natural beauty, from the scales of planets and stars to that of galaxies and large-scale structure.

This project is dedicated to the most arresting, most captivating, and most pioneering visualization efforts in computational astrophysics. The hope is to inspire and motivate work beyond the functional, and to capture the evolving language of design within the context of our humble corner of space.
